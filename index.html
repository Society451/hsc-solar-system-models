Eudoxus of Cnidus and the Homocentric Sphere Model

Eudoxus' model of celestial motion, based on five principles, was an elegant solution to explaining planetary and stellar movement around a fixed, central Earth. His model consisted of nested celestial spheres, each contributing to the observed motion of stars, planets, the sun, and the moon.

Key Principles of Eudoxus' Model

  1. The Earth is the center of the universe.
  2. All celestial motion is circular and regular.
  3. The path and center of each celestial motion are the same as that of its motion.
  4. The center of all celestial motion is Earth.
