Eudoxus' Planetary System

Ptolemy's Geocentric Model

Ptolemy's model was a geocentric model of the solar system where Earth is the center, and all celestial bodies move in complex circular paths involving deferents and epicycles.

Key Concepts

  • **Eccentric Circles**: Planets and the sun follow eccentric orbits that are offset from the Earth.
  • **Epicycles**: Circular paths within larger orbits to account for retrograde motion.
  • **Equant**: A point around which the planets move uniformly, even if they appear non-uniform from Earth.
